He aha te Matariki?

Matariki is a star cluster that appears in the early morning sky in New Zealand during the mid-winter months. According to the maramataka the reappearance of Matariki brings the old lunar year to a close and marks the beginning of the new year. Hence, Matariki is associated with the Maori New Year.

The Matariki star cluster, consists of 9 stars and it is well known throughout the world and at different times of the year can be seen around the globe. It is one of the brightest clusters in the sky, containing hundreds of member stars. Matariki has different names around the world. In English, it is called by its ancient Greek name, Pleiades or the Seven Sisters. In Hawaiian it is Makali’i, “eyes of royalty”, and in Japan it is called Subaru, meaning “gathered together

The exact calendar date of Matariki actually varies each year because it is based on lunar cycles, and different iwi begin their Matariki festivities at different times. Normally the matariki cluster is visible in Aotearoa skies in Hunae or Hurae ranei.

Te hiranga o Matariki

Matariki is known as the Maori New Year in Te Ao Maori. Closely connected with the maramataka, the reappearance of the Matariki stars in the early morning sky brings the past year to stop and marks the beginning of the new year. Matauranga Maori is at the heart of celebrations of the Matariki public holiday and it will be a time for, remembrance, celebrating the present, and looking for the future

Matariki is significant to New Zealand as a whole this is because historically, iwi would make their own ritual offerings to atua of land and horticulture, such as Uenuuku and Rongomatane. The visibility of Matariki made it fo the prime time of year to be making special offerings. Traditionally, such as occasion was valuable for accumulating knowledge of whenua and the remembrance of our tupuna

Māori would send their hopes and dreams into the stars during Matariki. Māori understood that lives depended on maintaining a strong connection to the physical world and caring for nature. This was a period for learning, sharing, discussion and decision making

Hei aha te whakanui i a Matariki

In the past, celebrating Matariki meant the coming together of families to remember ancesotrs, the lighting of ritual fires, sharing food, telling stories, signing and playing music. This idea or sentiment still hasn’t changed, Matariki is a great time to get together with family and friends or whanau and look towards a bright future, with the hope of a full kete. Matariki is also an ideal time to share and pass on aspects of culture and heritage.

Celebrating Matariki is an opportunity for all New Zealanders and people to honour our environment and to open ourselves to learning about the traditions of which go back hundreds of years beyond colonial settlement.

Celebrating Matariki is also a way to reunite ourselves with our friends and whanau, it is a time where we come together as one and bring the year that has passed to an end and welcome the upcoming year with all our heart and soul. Celebrating Matariki, is remembering our ancestors, celebrating the present, and looking for the future…
